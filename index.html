About Me

I’m Luis, a front-end developer and I’m passionate about combining the technical and visual aspects of digital products and bringing them to life.

I enjoy turning complex problems into simple, dynamic and intuitive designs whilst writing clean code.

My journey through coding started in 2017, after deciding that my job as a civil engineer wasn’t the right job for me, I decided to move to England to master my English.

This was a turning point in my life as a friend of mine introduced me to the ‘world’ of coding.

I initially helped my friend fixing small issues with clients websites using WordPress. These small issues varied from clear browser cache, deactivating installed plugins, fixing URLs, creating backups of websites, troubleshooting email issues etc.

At this point my curiosity for coding started to flourish and I decided to learn about CS101. I dived deep into coding and I started to learn about HTML and CSS, which allowed me to build some simple webpages.

My growing enthusiasm and enjoyment learning the new skills and technologies led me to expand my knowledge and enabled me to create interesting and innovative projects.

I started learning more about Git, GitHub, web hosting services, terminals, design tools, dev tools, JavaScript etc.

Whilst helping my friend, I realised that many of the skills that I learned as a civil engineer were directly applicable to web development, such as dealing with clients, working to tight deadlines, solving problems, setting clear goals, working in a team and providing great website performance and a reliable service.

I decided to become a full-time web developer and dedicate all of my time to improving my skills. For further information feel free to browse the projects that I have created.

In my spare time I like to cook for family and friends, play sports, meditate and read about topics such as space, philosophy, psychology and physics.
