About Me

I’m Luis, a DevOps engineer with a passion for automation, scalability, and building efficient CI/CD pipelines that streamline software delivery.

I enjoy turning complex problems into simple, dynamic and intuitive designs whilst writing clean code.

My journey into DevOps started with a background in front-end development, but my curiosity for infrastructure, cloud technologies, and automation led me to transition into this field.

I began my tech career in 2017 after moving to England to master my English and exploring coding as a new path. Initially, I worked on troubleshooting and maintaining WordPress websites, handling tasks such as clearing caches, managing plugins, fixing URLs, and resolving email issues. These experiences sparked my interest in problem-solving and system optimization.

As I deepened my knowledge, I expanded my skills into version control (Git/GitHub), web hosting, terminal commands, and cloud technologies. My interest in automation and infrastructure as code (IaC) led me to explore tools like Docker, Kubernetes, Terraform, and CI/CD systems such as Jenkins and GitHub Actions.

Transitioning from civil engineering to tech, I realized that many of the skills I developed—problem-solving, working with tight deadlines, collaborating with teams, and optimizing processes—are directly applicable to DevOps. Now, I thrive in environments where I can improve system reliability, automate repetitive tasks, and ensure smooth deployments.

For further details, feel free to check out my projects.

In my spare time, I enjoy cooking for family and friends, playing sports, meditating, and reading about space, philosophy, psychology, and physics.
